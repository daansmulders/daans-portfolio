What I learned from using Claude Code and SpecKit as primary tools:

**The workflow forces design thinking.** Writing a user story with an independent test criterion before touching code is just good design practice — SpecKit made it non-optional. I couldn't be lazy about what I was trying to achieve.

**The distance between decision and result collapsed.** "The dashboard is too chaotic" became a redesigned component in minutes. That speed changed how I thought — I could explore more directions, reject more things, and raise the bar on details I'd previously have left "for later".

**AI doesn't replace judgment.** The decisions that mattered — dashboard hierarchy, what to remove, how to handle the weekly milestone, tone of voice — were all deliberate. The tools executed them well. But without a clear design intent going in, faster execution would have just produced more noise faster.

The prototype isn't a finished product. It's a demonstration: of a domain worth solving, of how a two-sided medical app might feel, and of what a tighter loop between design thinking and working code looks like in practice.
