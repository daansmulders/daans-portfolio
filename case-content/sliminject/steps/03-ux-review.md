Once the initial build was working, I switched hats — ran a structured design review of every screen and turned the findings into 14 prioritised user stories.

I fed those stories into SpecKit with a role prompt: product owner reviewing a backlog. The workflow generated a formal spec, resolved technical unknowns through a research phase, produced a task breakdown, and then implemented all 14 improvements in sequence.

The most useful part wasn't the speed. It was the structure. Every user story required an independent test criterion before implementation could start. That constraint forced clarity: I couldn't be vague about what "better" meant.

The findings grouped into three areas: patient experience (habit formation, input clarity, expectations), doctor experience (navigation, visual feedback), and cross-cutting concerns (empty states, form feedback, tone of voice).
